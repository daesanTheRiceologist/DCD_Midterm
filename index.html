Fast fashion brings a negative impact to the environment. According to the internet, fast fashion creates 10% of the carbon dioxide pollution every year.

Fast fashion clothing is made with cheap, non-recyclable materials, this causes many countries to just burn the clothing which causes toxic chemicals going in the air we breathe in.

Scientists believe if fast fashion keeps going to pollution, soon it will take over 50% of the greenhouse gas emission. Not only that fast fashion is the second largest water consuming business.
During the couple years as fast fashion became more popular each year, clothing sales doubled from 100 billion to 200 billion units in the last few years.

Many of these unused clothes are just burnt which causes pollution or just left in junk yards left for years.
Labor issue is a big problem when it comes to fast fashion. People buy fast fashion because it is affordable and follows the latest trends.

But, most people forget about the labor issues that are behind when producing fast fashion products. Factory workers are constantly underpaid and working in dangerous environments during long hours of work.
Spreading your words about fast fashion and educating those around you can start a seed in stopping the fast fashion trend.

Remember that big changes happen from small ones and when you decide to make a change it is creating an even bigger change as you influence those around you.
Stop buying fast fashion completely. Don't support fast fashion brands so they stop growing.

Make your message to the fast fasihon brands by taking stand against them by not shopping their products.
Stop using and supporting fast fashion brands and instead use and support brands that are sustainable both clothing wise and environment wise.

Also, look for other options of getting clothes such as buying used clothes from those around you or thrifting.
